Climate change

Global warming and its major effects on water.

Sea Level Rise

Climate change contributes to the melting of glaciers and ice caps, leading to a rise in global sea levels. As sea levels rise, coastal areas become more vulnerable to flooding, saltwater intrusion into freshwater sources, and increased coastal erosion.

The numbers of ice masses like icebergs melting bit by bit only serves to make things worse. This also affects the fauna living in colder regions like the polar bears or seals. Without much terrain, some species migh even go extinct.

Changing Precipitation Patterns

Climate change alters precipitation patterns, leading to more intense and unpredictable rainfall events in some regions, while others experience prolonged droughts.

These changes can impact water availability, agricultural productivity, and freshwater ecosystems.

The warmer the climate gets, the more scarce all resources become. As of years ago, efforts against globale warming cannot afford to dwindle.

Melting Mountain Glaciers

Mountain glaciers act as natural water storage, releasing meltwater during dry seasons to sustain rivers and ecosystems downstream.

Climate change causes the accelerated melting of these glaciers, disrupting the natural flow of water and affecting water supplies for communities and ecosystems that rely on them.

Such results are already showing in northern countries like Norway, from the lack of ice, reduction in snow accumulation and unpredictable weather.
